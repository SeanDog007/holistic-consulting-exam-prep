### 🔴 FACTUAL ERROR (1)

#### D1-Q280 (global index in Domain 1)
**Q:** "Professional health organizations such, as the American Heart Association, and the Dietary Guidelines for Americans 2015–2025 recommend that people consume _________ of calories per day from added sugar."
**Answer:** less than 10% ✓ (correct)
**Issue:** "Dietary Guidelines for Americans 2015–2025" is not a real edition. The editions are **2015–2020** and **2020–2025**. This is a factual error in the question stem.
**Recommended fix:** Change to "Dietary Guidelines for Americans 2020–2025" or simply "Dietary Guidelines for Americans"
**Also:** Remove the comma after "such" — "such, as" should be "such as"
**Priority:** LOW — doesn't affect the answer, but a student could flag it.

---

### 🟡 AMBIGUOUS (1)

#### D1-Q76 (global index in Domain 1)
**Q:** "What are sources of heme iron?"
- A: poultry, salmon, beef, and eggs ✓
- B: spinach, oatmeal, nuts, and seeds

**Issue:** Eggs contain predominantly **non-heme iron** (~93% non-heme). While eggs are an animal product and the pedagogical intent is clearly "animal vs. plant" sources, technically eggs are a poor source of heme iron. In nutrition science, eggs are routinely categorized as a non-heme iron source.
**Verdict:** The answer is defensible in context (animal grouping vs. plant grouping), but a well-read student might challenge this.
**Recommended fix (optional):** Replace "eggs" with "lamb" or "shellfish" in option A.
**Priority:** LOW-MEDIUM — needs SME signoff if changing.
