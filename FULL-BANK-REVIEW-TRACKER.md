### 🟡 AMBIGUOUS (1)

#### D1-Q76 (global index in Domain 1)
**Q:** "What are sources of heme iron?"
- A: poultry, salmon, beef, and eggs ✓
- B: spinach, oatmeal, nuts, and seeds

**Issue:** Eggs contain predominantly **non-heme iron** (~93% non-heme). While eggs are an animal product and the pedagogical intent is clearly "animal vs. plant" sources, technically eggs are a poor source of heme iron. In nutrition science, eggs are routinely categorized as a non-heme iron source.
**Verdict:** The answer is defensible in context (animal grouping vs. plant grouping), but a well-read student might challenge this.
**Recommended fix (optional):** Replace "eggs" with "lamb" or "shellfish" in option A.
**Priority:** LOW-MEDIUM — needs SME signoff if changing.

### 🔴 FACTUAL ERRORS (4) — URGENT

#### D2-Q72 — WRONG ANSWER KEY
**Q:** "An enzyme in the stomach that can hydrolyze protein is:"
**Options:** A: trypsin | B: bicarbonate | C: enterokinase
**Current answer:** 0 (trypsin)
**Issue:** **Trypsin is a PANCREATIC enzyme, NOT a stomach enzyme.** The stomach protease is pepsin. None of the given options is correct for this question. Trypsin acts in the small intestine.
**Recommended fix:** Replace option A with "pepsin" and keep answer as 0, OR rewrite the question entirely.
**Priority:** 🔴 HIGH — factually wrong answer key. Students will learn incorrect information.

#### D2-Q104 — INTERNAL INCONSISTENCY (contradicts D1-Q24)
**Q:** "How many amino acids are considered essential?"
**Options:** A: 6 | B: 7 | C: 8
**Current answer:** 2 (8)
**Issue:** Domain 1 Q24 asks the same question and answers **9** (correct per modern nutrition science — the 9 essential amino acids include histidine). This question says 8. **The bank contradicts itself.** Modern consensus = 9 essential amino acids.
**Recommended fix:** Change option C to "9" and keep as correct answer. Alternatively, add option D: "9" and set it as correct.
**Priority:** 🔴 HIGH — students get conflicting info depending on which exam they take.

#### D2-Q135 — WRONG ANSWER KEY
**Q:** "Which of the following is considered a carotenoid?"
**Options:** A: Retinol | B: Retinal | C: Retinoic acid | D: All answers are correct
**Current answer:** 3 ("All answers are correct")
**Issue:** **Retinol, retinal, and retinoic acid are RETINOIDS (preformed vitamin A), NOT carotenoids.** Carotenoids are plant pigments (beta-carotene, lycopene, lutein, etc.) that serve as vitamin A precursors. This is a fundamental distinction in nutrition biochemistry.
**Recommended fix:** Replace options with actual carotenoids (e.g., A: beta-carotene, B: lycopene, C: lutein, D: All), OR change the question to "Which of the following is considered a retinoid?"
**Priority:** 🔴 HIGH — teaches incorrect biochemistry classification.

#### D2-Q139 — FACTUAL ERROR IN QUESTION STEM
**Q:** "Which nutrient is an anticoagulant and supports the synthesis of the bone proteins Gla protein (osteocalcin) and matrix Gla protein (MGP)?"
**Answer:** Vitamin K ✓ (correct for bone proteins)
**Issue:** The question stem says "anticoagulant" — **Vitamin K is a PRO-coagulant.** It is essential for blood clotting (synthesizes clotting factors II, VII, IX, X). Warfarin works by BLOCKING vitamin K specifically because vitamin K promotes coagulation. The word "anticoagulant" is 100% backward.
**Recommended fix:** Change "anticoagulant" to "essential for blood clotting" or "pro-coagulant."
**Priority:** 🔴 HIGH — teaches the opposite of the truth about vitamin K's clotting role.

### 🟡 AMBIGUOUS (5)

#### D2-Q57
**Q:** "What is the name for the type of basophil that takes up residence along blood vessels?"
**Answer:** Mast cells ✓
**Issue:** Mast cells are NOT technically "a type of basophil." They share a common myeloid progenitor but are distinct cell lineages. In simplified nutrition education this is defensible, but immunology students would flag it.
**Verdict:** LOW risk — leave as-is or add a note. No student is likely to fail over this.

#### D2-Q68
**Q:** "Which of the following structures allows blood to first enter the small and large intestine as well as the pancreas?"
**Answer:** Hepatic portal vein
**Issue:** The hepatic portal vein DRAINS blood FROM the GI organs TO the liver. It does not deliver blood to the intestines. The question wording is backward. Also: "Sphincter of Ohdi" → "Sphincter of Oddi" (typo).
**Recommended fix:** Rewrite: "Which vessel carries blood AWAY FROM the GI tract to the liver?" Also fix Oddi spelling.
**Priority:** MEDIUM — answer is the right vessel but the question logic is reversed.

#### D2-Q73
**Q:** "Gallbladder bile has the greatest concentration of which of the following compounds?"
**Answer:** Bilirubin
**Issue:** Bile salts are the dominant component of bile (not an option). Among the given choices, bile contains more cholesterol than bilirubin. The answer is debatable.
**Verdict:** LOW-MEDIUM risk. Needs SME input.

#### D2-Q116
**Q:** "Which is the most prevalent saturated fatty acid in the Western diet?"
**Options:** A: Palmitic acid | B: Stearic acid | C: Both are common
**Answer:** 2 ("Both are common")
**Issue:** Palmitic acid is clearly the most prevalent saturated FA in Western diets (~60% of dietary SFA). The question asks for "most prevalent" — "both are common" dodges the question.
**Verdict:** LOW risk — not wrong, but imprecise pedagogy.

#### D2-Q127
**Q:** "Where is connective tissue, bone, cartilage, and tendons composed?"
**Answer:** Golgi apparatus
**Issue:** Confusing wording. The Golgi apparatus processes/packages proteins but doesn't "compose" connective tissue. Collagen synthesis starts in the rough ER, is processed in Golgi, and assembled extracellularly. The question is poorly phrased.
**Verdict:** LOW-MEDIUM risk. Defensible in simplified context.

#### D2-Q178
**Q:** "Which of the following is a function of the trigeminal nerve?"
**Answer:** "It extends into the brain from the face and forehead and plays a role in inhibiting seizures."
**Issue:** The trigeminal nerve's primary functions are facial sensation and motor control of mastication. "Inhibiting seizures" refers to experimental trigeminal nerve stimulation (TNS) therapy — not a standard anatomical function.
**Verdict:** LOW risk in holistic nutrition context, but could confuse students taking anatomy courses.

### 🟡 AMBIGUOUS (2)

#### D3-Q36 — CBT described as "changing the environment"
**Q:** "What type of therapy is focused on changing the environment?"
**Answer:** Cognitive Behavioral Therapy ✓
**Issue:** CBT is primarily focused on changing **thoughts and behaviors**, not the environment. "Changing the environment" better describes **stimulus control** or **environmental restructuring** techniques. However, CBT does include behavioral components that modify environmental cues, and the other options (Countering, Journaling) are worse fits.
**Verdict:** LOW risk — defensible in a simplified curriculum. Could reword stem to "changing harmful thoughts and behaviors" for precision.
**Priority:** LOW — no student will fail over this, but it's technically imprecise.

#### D3-Q44 — Reflective listening listed as nonverbal behavior
**Q:** "What is an example of effective counseling nonverbal behavior?"
**Options:** A: Good posture | B: Eye contact | C: Reflective listening | D: All answers are correct
**Current answer:** 3 ("All answers are correct")
**Issue:** **Reflective listening is a VERBAL technique** — it involves paraphrasing and restating what the client said. It is not nonverbal behavior. Good posture and eye contact are genuinely nonverbal, but reflective listening is not.
**Recommended fix:** Replace option C "Reflective listening" with "Head nodding" or "Facial expressions" — both are genuinely nonverbal. Keep answer as "All answers are correct."
**Priority:** MEDIUM — a well-prepared student could correctly argue this answer is wrong.
