Online Delivery

The BCHN exam is delivered online through ProctorU. Questions are randomly selected from the question bank, so every exam experience is unique. Question types include multiple choice, true/false, short answer, and matching.

Domain weights: Food & Nutrition (35%), A&P/Biochemistry (15%), Counseling/Ethics/Scope (10%), Nutrition in Practice (30%), Research (10%).

Test-Taking Strategies

Multiple Choice

  • Read the question stem completely before looking at answers
  • Eliminate obviously wrong answers first — increases your odds
  • Watch for absolute words ("always," "never") — these are usually wrong
  • If two answers are similar, one of them is likely correct
  • "All of the above" is correct more often than "none of the above"

Time Management

  • Pace: roughly 1 minute per question
  • Don't spend more than 2 minutes on any single question
  • Flag difficult questions and come back if time allows
  • Trust your first instinct — changed answers are wrong more often

Short Answer & Matching

  • For matching: start with the terms you're most confident about
  • For short answer: be concise and specific — they're looking for key terms

The 48-Hour Protocol

Two Days Before

  • Do a light review of your weakest domain — don't cram new material
  • Review your flashcard deck one final time
  • Set up your ProctorU environment (quiet room, clear desk, webcam, stable internet)
  • Test your computer and internet connection with ProctorU's system check

The Night Before

  • Stop studying by early evening — no late-night cramming
  • Prepare your ID and any allowed materials
  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep
  • Avoid alcohol and heavy meals

Morning Of

  • Eat a balanced breakfast with protein and complex carbs
  • Avoid excessive caffeine — it can increase anxiety
  • Log in 15 minutes early to get settled
  • Take 3 slow, deep breaths before starting
  • Remember: you've prepared for this. Trust your training.

Managing Test Anxiety

Test anxiety is normal and manageable. Here are evidence-based techniques:

  • Box breathing: Inhale 4 seconds → hold 4 seconds → exhale 4 seconds → hold 4 seconds. Repeat 3-4 times.
  • 5-4-3-2-1 grounding: Name 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
  • Positive self-talk: Replace "I don't know this" with "I've studied this and I'll do my best."
  • Progress reframe: If you hit a hard question, remind yourself of the ones you've already answered confidently.
